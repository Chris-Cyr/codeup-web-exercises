Yosemite

  • For 3,000 years, the park has been inhabited and was visited as long ago as 10,000 years!
  • The highest free climb in history took place on the face of the park's granite escarpment "El Capitan"
  • Contains 160 acres of glaciers
  • Films as varied as "Star Trek V: The Final Frontier", "The Last of the Mohicans", and "Maverick" were filmed in Yosemite!

YellowStone

  • 11,000 years ago Native Americans began to hunt in the park.
  • In 1805 the famous Lewis and Clark Expedition connected the European settlers of the Eastern US to the ancient Native American cultures in the park.
  • 1872 was a banner year for Yellowstone when President Ulysses S. Grant signed The Act of Dedication which officially recognized Yellowstone as a national park.
  • In 1915 there was a convergence of eras when horse drawn carriages were outlawed due to the thousands of automobiles which began traversing the park each year.

Mesa Verde

  • Nomadic Paleo-Indians settled the Mesa Verde Valley as early as 7500 BC
  • Native Peoples carved houses and living quarters into cliff-sides which still remain in Mesa Verde
  • Mexican-Spanish missionaries re-discovered the long-abandoned ruins of the park in 1776
  • Housing structures contain shale that was a part of the Cretaceous Sea which had completely receded to leave the young rocks.
